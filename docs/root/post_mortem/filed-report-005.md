# FIELD REPORT #005: INTERSTATE BLOWOUT
**SUBJECT:** REAR PASSENGER TIRE (32K MILES ON A RATED 50K CARCASS)
**STATUS:** RESOLVED // SPARE IN SERVICE, REPLACEMENT ORDERED
**LOCATION:** I-90, MILE MARKER UNKNOWN, SOMEWHERE DRY

### THE INCIDENT
Tread separation at approximately 62mph, announced by a sound best described as a gunshot wrapped in a drumroll. No loss of control — the extra mass over the rear axle actually helped here, the van settled rather than swerved. Pulled onto the shoulder over about 200 yards.

### DAMAGE ASSESSMENT
- **Tire:** Total loss, tread fully separated from the carcass.
- **Fender well:** Minor cosmetic scarring from flying rubber.
- **Wiring harness routed through that wheel well:** Inspected, undamaged, but noted as a vulnerability for future routing decisions.

### RECOVERY & DIAGNOSTICS
Spare deployment was uneventful — jack points had been marked and tested during a calm-weather dry run months prior, which is the only reason this took 20 minutes instead of an hour of guessing. The failed tire's age (6 years) exceeded its mileage-implied lifespan; rubber degrades on a calendar, not just an odometer.

### CITIZEN SCIENTIST VERDICT
Dry-run your emergency procedures before you need them under pressure. The jack points were marked in chalk from a practice session in the driveway — that chalk mark is the entire reason this was boring instead of a disaster.

> **Lesson:** Tires die of old age even when the tread looks fine. Check the date code, not just the depth.
